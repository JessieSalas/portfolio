Featured Project

Patent Fox

I developed the full interface for Patent Fox: an app designed to leverage the power of Jeapordy-winning IBM Watson's cognitive computing capability to create a better patent search tool. Patent Fox was developed in a team of UC Berkeley business and technical students as part of the 2014 IBM Watson university competition, winning 1st place in the UC Berkeley competition, and 3rd overall in the international competition.

Clear Actions

The app is designed to make clear the actions that the app wants from the user, and to enable the user to interact with the app in a swift and concise way to achieve the app's purpose.

Fox Logo

I designed the fox logo, which resembles a book to connote the information contained in the patents the app searches.

Symba

A partner and I co-developed a music practice tool called Symba, which stands for Symbol-analysis; It's an approach to learning jazz improvisation by methodically practicing each chord symbol. We are currently collaborating on pursuing a web implementation and increased functionality.

Symba works by using proven learning techniques to break down music-learning into a measurable science, so that users can track their progress over time, something very difficult or impossible to do using learning techniques of the past.

Etude

Etude is an award-winning software which automates the creation of human-playable etudes, based on an implementation of text-parsing techniques and music theory to decide optimal notes to play over given chord changes.

Etude uses standard jazz theory to find "good" notes to write over certain changes, also using probablity to better simulate human-written pieces. The user just selects which jazz song they want to practice, and etude delivers a personalized sheet of music based on an increasing number of preferences.

Fil

I co-created Fil, a mobile phone keyboard which implements frame-semantics-based text prediction using the FrameNet dataset, based on the research of Charles Fillmore at ICSI and UCB.

This results in text prediction that is more accurate than naive ngram/statistical approaches because it is based on a context of associated words rather than just neighboring strings.
